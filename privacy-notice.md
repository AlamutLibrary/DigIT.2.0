# Privacy Notice — Alamut Library

**Last updated:** April 2026  
**Data controller:** Aslisho Qurboniev  
**Contact:** [aqurboniev@iis.ac.uk@iis.ac.uk](mailto:aqurboniev@iis.ac.uk@iis.ac.uk)

---

## 1. Who we are

The Alamut Library (DigIT.2.0) is a digital research tool developed and maintained by Aslisho Qurboniev (IIS). It provides access to a corpus of Arabic and Persian Ismaili texts and AI-assisted reading and research functions.

---

## 2. What data we collect

When you use the Alamut Library, we collect the following:

| Data | Purpose | Retention |
|---|---|---|
| **Search and chat queries** | To understand how the tool is used and improve retrieval quality | 12 months |
| **Country of access** (derived from IP address) | To understand the geographic reach of the library | 12 months |
| **Timestamp** | To analyse usage patterns | 12 months |
| **AI provider selected** | To understand tool preferences | 12 months |
| **Search mode** (RAG / keyword / full text) | To evaluate and improve retrieval methods | 12 months |
| **Approximate query length** | To understand research patterns | 12 months |

We do **not** collect or store:

- Your name or email address
- Your full IP address (only country-level location is retained)
- Any personal information you may include in a query
- API keys you enter in the sidebar (these are stored only in your browser session and never transmitted to our logging system)

---

## 3. Lawful basis for processing

We process this data under **Article 6(1)(f) of the UK GDPR — Legitimate Interests**. Our legitimate interest is the improvement of a specialist academic research tool serving the scholarly study of Ismaili thought, history, and literature. We have assessed that this interest is not overridden by your rights and freedoms, given:

- The data collected is minimal and pseudonymous
- Full IP addresses are not retained
- The tool serves an academic, non-commercial purpose
- Users are clearly informed of data collection via this notice

---

## 4. How we use your data

Data collected is used exclusively to:

- Understand which texts and topics researchers are most interested in
- Identify gaps in the corpus or retrieval quality
- Improve the AI-assisted search and chat functions
- Report aggregate usage statistics to IIS for institutional reporting

We do **not** use your data for:

- Commercial purposes
- Advertising or profiling
- Sale or transfer to third parties
- Automated decision-making that affects you

---

## 5. Who has access to your data

Access to usage logs is restricted to:

- The developer (Aslisho Qurboniev, IIS)
- The IIS Data Protection Officer, for compliance purposes

Data is stored on **Cloudflare's infrastructure** (Workers KV or D1), which is subject to Cloudflare's data processing agreement. Cloudflare may store data in the US and EEA under standard contractual clauses.

---

## 6. Your rights

Under UK GDPR you have the right to:

- **Access** the data we hold about you
- **Erasure** — request deletion of your data
- **Restriction** — ask us to limit processing
- **Object** — object to processing based on legitimate interests
- **Portability** — receive your data in a machine-readable format
- **Complain** to the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO): [ico.org.uk](https://ico.org.uk) / 0303 123 1113

To exercise any of these rights, contact: [aqurboniev@iis.ac.uk](mailto:aqurboniev@iis.ac.uk)

Because we do not collect identifying information, we may not be able to locate records relating to a specific individual without additional information from you.

---

## 7. Cookies and local storage

The Alamut Library uses **browser session storage** only to temporarily store your API key within your current browser session. This is not a cookie and is not transmitted to any server. It is cleared when you close your browser tab.

We do not use tracking cookies or third-party analytics (e.g. Google Analytics).
